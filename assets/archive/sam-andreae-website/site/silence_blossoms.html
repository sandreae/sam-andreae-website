silence blossoms

For this band I've spent much time working with poetry and lyrics to write songs that the band can use as a starting point for improvisations. Sometimes we work with a particular idea for a song (a texture, combination of instruments or rhythmic pattern) and sometimes we leave the whole process very open, letting the compositions merge into each other. The band is Hanna Olivegren (voice) and Gus Loxbo (bass, guitar, saw, voice) and myself (saxophone, clarinet, electronics, keyboard, voice).
We play mostly in Sweden but sometimes in Norway and Denmark too. We've been selling lo-fi recordings at gigs for a while now, but we are planning a combined cassette tape and digital release of live material later in 2012.
This is one of the poems we've used, and artwork from Angela Guyton, who is our expressive translator in all things visual-art related.


POEM
I heard of a man
who says words so beautifully
that if he only speaks their name
women give themselves to him
If I am dumb beside your body
while silence blossoms like tumors on our lips
it is because I hear a man climb stairs
and clear his throat outside our door
Leonard Cohen
